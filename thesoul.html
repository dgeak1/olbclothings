The Dye Pits of Itoku

Ancient Blue Gold:
The Art of Adire

Every OLB piece starts with heritage. Our Adire is hand-dyed using centuries-old resist-dyeing techniques. It is a slow, rhythmic process that cannot be rushed by machines.

The Loom

Ofi Heritage:
The King of Cloths

We work with master weavers in Oyo State to create Aso-Oke (Ofi) that maintains its structural integrity while gaining a modern silhouette. It is the armor of the Yoruba elite, reimagined for the global citizen.

The Atelier Manifesto

01 / Slow Fashion

We do not follow seasons. We follow the rhythm of the loom and the hand of the craftsman.

02 / Cultural Integrity

Respect for the silhouette. We don't just use African prints; we honor African structures.

03 / Ibadan Heart

Every stitch is placed within our private workshop in the historic city of Ibadan.
